# About Me

Hello, I'm Semih. The Drivionline project started in the early months of 2025, driven by my interest in Godot and my passion for game development. In the beginning, I began by researching and examining various projects and ideas. My primary interest was in vehicle simulations and driving games.

Driven by this curiosity, I discovered jreo03's VitaVehicle - "Realistic" Car Physics (g-rcp2/RCP4) project and began experimenting with it to kickstart my first project. After exploring jreo03's GitHub page (https://github.com/jreo03/g-rcp2), I started my own development journey in Godot. I initially focused on building my own menu and settings systems.

Later, I shifted my focus toward economy and multiplayer systems. My original vision was to create an economy-driven world using Node.js-based API systems. However, I eventually realized this wasn't the right path for the project. Instead, I decided to make the game free and pivoted toward developing a private server system and a Lua-based modding system.

Since Drivionline is currently in active development, the core mechanics continue to be shaped and optimized based on community feedback. I am still developing the Node.js-based infrastructure for session and account management, while using C# and Lua for the game server and modding systems.

Initially, I considered implementing an in-game purchase system with microtransactions. However, after seeing the legal complexities, business overhead, and the requirements for selling digital assets, I moved away from that model. I chose to embrace a more transparent, development-friendly, and modding-oriented approach, leading to the decision to keep the game completely free.

To be honest, the main inspiration I strive to follow is LFS (Live For Speed). I have played LFS for a long time and truly admire its physics, optimization, and simplicity—especially considering it wasn't developed by a massive corporation. That said, I must admit that Drivionline's physics are not yet as deep as LFS's 20 years of expertise and currently follow a simpler approach.

If you have any questions or suggestions, feel free to reach out via the game's social media accounts or by sending an email.
